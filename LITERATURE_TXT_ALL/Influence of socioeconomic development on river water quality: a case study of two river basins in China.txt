RESEARCH ARTICLE
Influence of socioeconomic development on river water quality:
a case study of two river basins in China
Shiwei Yang1 & Hongsheng Hao2 & Bing Liu 3 & Yuanming Wang1 & Yongan Yang4 & Ruifeng Liang1 & Kefeng Li 1
Received: 7 January 2021 / Accepted: 4 May 2021
# The Author(s), under exclusive licence to Springer-Verlag GmbH Germany, part of Springer Nature 2021
Abstract
Social and economic development processes require large amounts of natural resources and in some cases seriously
deteriorate river water quality. Since the reform and expansion era began, China has vigorously pursued socioeconomic
development but neglected environmental protection. Howeve r, in recent years, improvements in environmental aware-
ness and the implementation of environmental protection meas ures have led to a balanced relationship between economic
development and the environment. In this study, the Yangtze River Basin and the Yellow River Basin were selected as
research areas. We used a combination of canonical correlation analysis (CCA) and a distance- based influence assessment
method to quantitatively assess the influence of socioeconomi c development on river water quality. The results revealed a
strong correlation between socioeconomi c development and river water quality. Th e degree of influence of socioeconomic
development on water quality varied not only temporally but also spatially due to differences in socioeconomic develop-
ment and hydrometeorology in the two basins in North and So uth China. The average degree of influence in the Yangtze
River Basin was between 0.22 and 0.27, and that in the Yellow River Basin was between 0.2 and 0.36. Moreover, the
degree of influence in the Yangtze River Basin in the wet season was greater than that in the dry season, whereas the
opposite pattern was observed in the Yellow River Basin. The degree of influence in both basins gradually declined after
2011, indicating that the coupling and coordination between socioeconomic development and environmental protection
have continuously improved and that the water quality has g radually improved. By analysing the influences of various
socioeconomic indicators on water quality, we found that the main factors that influence water quality are per capita GDP
and urbanization rate in the Yangtze River Basin and urbanization rate in the Yellow River Basin. The results provide a
basis for future sustainable development in the Yangtze River Basin and the Yellow River Basin.
Keywords Socioeconomic development. Water quality . Degree of influence . CCA . Yangtze River Basin . Yellow River Basin
Introduction
Urbanization is an inevitable trend of the development of hu-
man society and a common trend of economic development
(Wang & Zhao 2009). Currently, more than 55% of the
world’ s population lives in urban areas (UNDESA, 2018).
Industrialization promotes urbanization, and urbanization
serves as the spatial foundation on which the sustainable de-
velopment of industrialization depends (Xie 2020). The for-
mation of urban agglomerations is a process through which
industries constantly develop, transform and upgrade.
Industrial vicissitudes impact spatial forms and structures,
and the geographic concentration and agglomeration of indus-
tries drive changes in production space and together affect the
spatial evolution of urban agglomeration (Niu et al. 2020). In
the process of urbanization, a large number of people move to

In the past few decades, China has achieved large-scale
urbanization, with a large number of people moving from
rural areas to cities. The urban area and urban population
density have increased year by year. Since reform and expan-
sion began in the late 1970s, according to the National Bureau
of Statistics of China (NBSC) ( 2017), China ’ su r b a n i z a t i o n
rate has increased from 17.92% in 1978 to 57.35% in 2017.
China’ s average urbanization rate is approximately 2.45 times
that of the world over the same period (Ren et al. 2018). It is
estimated that by 2030, China’ s urban population will reach 1
billion, and the urbanization rate will reach 70%
(Development Research Center of the State Council 2014).
With the world’ s largest developing country and the world ’ s
second largest economy, China has had a far-reaching influ-
ence on global development (Wu et al. 2019).
However, urbanization has not only yielded positive bene-
fits for social and economic development in China but also led
to adverse effects to the ecological environment. The urbani-
zation process requires a large amount of natural resources
and can lead to water shortages, biodiversity loss, environ-
mental destruction and other problems (Chang & Zhu 2020,
Wang et al. 2019, Zhu et al. 2020). Increasing populations and
rapid economic development have intensified basin water re-
source use and destroyed the original hydrological system.
Additionally, the water deman ds for agriculture, industry
and urban living have increased, and sewage emissions have
intensified, further deteriorating the quality of river water.
Agricultural water use results in irrigation wastewater, indus-
trial water use produces large amounts of industrial wastewa-
ter, and the domestic water use produces a large amount of
domestic sewage. All of these wastewater types increase the
pollution load in rivers and worsen river water quality (Gong
et al. 2015). These phenomena have generated severe chal-
lenges related to Chinese resources and the environmental
carrying capacity (Liao et al. 2020, Yigitcanlar &
Kamruzzaman 2015). All these factors resulted from
neglecting to consider environmental protection in the process
of urbanization and reflect a correlation between the water
environment and human activities (Li et al. 2013).
In recent years, with economic development, the govern-
ment has taken corresponding environmental protection mea-
sures to improve water quality. The State Council released the
Action Plan for the Prevention and Control of Water Pollution
(Water 10) in 2015, aiming to improve the water quality of the
country ’ s ten largest river basins, with the main goal of
reaching good water quality levels in 70% of the seven major
river basins by 2020 and in 75% by 2030 (State Council in
China 2015). These environmental protection measures can
improve water quality by increasing the centralized sewage
treatment rate in cities and controlling diffuse pollution in
rural areas. Compared with purely considering economic
growth alone, relevant environmental protection policies can
effectively reduce water pollution and improve the negative
influence of economic development on the environment
(Wang & Wei 2019). As described by the environmental
Kuznets curve, in the early stages of economic growth, pollu-
tion emissions increase, and environmental quality declines,
but as per capita income levels rise, economic growth leads to
environmental improvements (Shahbaz et al. 2020).
Many studies
have focused on the effects of social and
economic development on water quality, and some notable
studies have focused on the influence of various human activ-
ities (urbanization, land use, dam building, etc.) and socioeco-
nomic development on river water quality (Cheng et al.
2018a, Deng et al. 2016, Liu et al. 2017, Luo et al. 2018,
Luo et al. 2020, Vrebos et al. 2017). The applied research
methods can be roughly divided into the following categories:
(1) simple statistical methods (O ’ Donoghue et al. 2021;T u
2011), (2) watershed models (Bussi et al. 2016), (3) multiple
regression models (Eslamian et al. 2016)a n d( 4 )c a n o n i c a l
correlation analysis methods (Wei et al. 2018). The above
methods have distinct advantages and disadvantages. A
simple statistical method can only qualitatively analyse the
correlations between variables; it cannot quantitatively
analyse them. Modelling m ethods based on physical
foundations have high precision but require considerable
data support and complex calculations. Multiple regression
models are suitable for identifying relationships between one
variable and multiple others but not for identifying
relationships between two sets of multivariate data. CCA is
a good choice for studying relationships between two sets of
multivariate data. This method does not require high data
accuracy and is simple to perform. Luo et al. ( 2019) first
proposed the combination of CCA and distance-based influ-
ence assessment methods to quantitatively study the effects of
various socioeconomic indicators on water quality. In this
study, we applied this approach to basins in China to analyse
differences in the degree of influence of socioeconomic devel-
opment in two typical basins in China and the year-by-year
changes in the degree of influence.
River basins function as complete hydrological units that
provide important support for aquatic ecosystems, and they
also support regional economic development. When the pol-
lution generated by development is not adequately controlled,
the water environment is degraded (Xian et al. 2007). Most
research on this topic has concentrated on comparisons and
analyses of water quality within a single river basin (Liu2015;
Wang et al. 2016). However, China is a large country, and the
level of economic development varies greatly among river
basins. The differences between the north and the south of

the country are particularly significant. The Yangtze River,
located in the south of China, is the third longest river in the
world. The economic development and urbanization process-
es in the Yangtze River Economic Belt in this basin have been
rapid, and increases in the population and economic level have
significantly influenced the water quality of the rivers in this
basin (Liu et al. 2020b,P e n ge ta l .2020). The Yellow River,
located in the north of China, is the second longest river in
China and the fifth longest river in the world, but the GDP in
the Yellow River Basin is lower than that in the Yangtze River
Basin. Due to the lagged nature of economic system reform
and the outflow of the northern labour force, the regional
economic development level between northern and southern
China is unbalanced (Zhang et al. 2019).
We divided the study period into two stages, namely, the
wet season (July to September) and the dry season (January to
March), to study the influence of socioeconomic development
on the water quality in the Yangtze River Basin and the
Yellow River Basin from 2005 to 2017. This study is the first
to investigate the relationships between socioeconomic factors
and water quality in these two important basins in China. The
correlations between the socioeconomic system and the river
system in these basins were calculated, and the impacts of the
socioeconomic system on water quality were assessed. The
main socioeconomic factors that influence river system chang-
es were identified. The results of this study can inform the
coordinated development of economic and environmental
protection in the two basins in the future.
Materials and methods
Study area
The watershed map is shown in Fig. 1. Located between 90–
122° east longitude and 24 –35° north latitude, the Yangtze
River Basin covers an area of 1.8 million square kilometres,
accounting for 18.8% of China’ s total land area. The Yangtze
River Basin is mainly located in a subtropical and temperate
climate zone. The average annual precipitation in the region is
approximately 1100 mm, and this precipitation mainly occurs
in summer. The annual mean air temperature is approximately
14 °C (Xiao et al. 2020). The main channel of the Yangtze
River is 6300 km long and spans 19 provinces in China and
across three economic zones in eastern, central and western
China. The Yangtze River Economic Belt is the most power-
ful social and economic area in China. As a major national
strategic development zone, this area is also an inland river
economic belt of global influence (the State Council about
relying on the golden waterway to promote the development
of the Yangtze River Economic Belt, 2019). The Yangtze
Economic Belt covers three state-level city clusters: the
Yangtze River Delta Urban Agglomerations, the Triangle of
Central China and the Chengdu-Chongqing City Group. In
2015, the population of the Yangtze River Economic Belt
was 6.16 million, accounting for 40% of the national popula-
tion, and the regional GDP was approximately $4.12 trillion,
accounting for 42.3% of the total national GDP.
The Yellow River Basin is located between 96 –119° east
longitude and 32 –42° north latitude, covering an area of
795,000 km
2, most of which are located in semiarid and arid
climate zones. The long-term annual mean precipitation and
runoff in the whole basin are approximately 466 and 73 mm,
respectively, with a low runoff coefficient of less than 0.16.
The annual mean temperature in the Yellow River Basin
varies from 8 to 14 °C, with a relatively high water demand
for evapotranspiration (Ma et al. 2020). With a total length of
5464 km and a drop of 4480 m, the Yellow River flows
through nine provinces of China. This river is an important
water source for Northwest and North China. In 2015, the
resident population of the Yellow River Basin was approxi-
mately 1.30 million, accounting for 9.1% of the national pop-
ulation, and the total GDP was approximately $0.94 trillion,
accounting for only 9.28% of the national GDP. Due to his-
torical and natural conditions in the basin, economic develop-
ment in the Yellow River Basin has been relatively lagged,
and there is an obvious gap between the development level
there and that in the Yangtze River Basin area.
Data collection
Socioeconomic indicators
In this study, the social and economic indicators are based on
data from the National Bureau of Statistics ( https://data.stats.
gov.cn/) and the statistical yearbooks of the provinces. The
indicators used to measure social and economic
development are mainly divided into three categories:
population urbanization, economic urbanization and spatial
urbanization. Considering the urbanization and environment
in the Yangtze River Basin and Yellow River Basin, per capita
GDP, urbanization rate and population density are selected as
the socioeconomic indicators (Guo et al. 2015). These indica-
tors represent the level of socioeconomic development and
take into account both urban and rural development. The data
for these indicators cover the period from 2005 to 2017.
Urbanization rate is a measure of the urbanization level, per
capita GDP measures the regional level of macroeconomic
development, and the population density is a landmark indi-
cator of the population distribution in a country or region.
For the selected timescale, the selected socioeconomic in-
dicators (percent change, amount per capita, etc.) are quite
stable over a year and therefore are considered to be evenly
distributed over time. In this study, we mainly studied the wet
season and dry season. The wet season is from June to
September, and the dry season is from January to March.

Water quality indicators
The water quality indicator data used in this study are from the
water environment quality data set of the Ministry of Ecology
and Environment Data Center (https://datacenter.mee.gov.cn/
websjzx/queryIndex.vm). The data set mainly includes pH,
dissolved oxygen (DO), the permanganate index (COD
Mn)
and NH 4-N data. In this study, we selected 6 monitoring
sections along the main stem of the Yangtze River, which
we denoted as YP1 –YP6. Moreover, we selected four
monitoring sections along the main stem of the Yellow
River, labelled HP1 –HP4 (Fig. 1). Based on the data from
the monitored sections, we analysed the temporal and spatial
variations in water quality in each basin under the influence of
socioeconomic development, as well as the difference in the
degree of influence between the two basins.
The timescale of water quality data used in this study was
from 2005 to 2017, and data were observed weekly. The qua-
dratic interpolation method was used for missing data. To
unify the timescale of the water quality data and the socioeco-
nomic indicator data, we adopted the mean value of the water
quality data during the wet season and the dry season.

The core steps in CCA and distance-based influence as-
sessment methods can be simplified as follows: (1) calculate
the degree of influence of each water quality indicator by
using the distance-based influence assessment method; (2)
use the CCA method to calculate the canonical correlation
coefficient between social and economic development sys-
tems and water quality systems; (3) based on the CCA meth-
od, the eigenvalue (used to calculate the weights of socioeco-
nomic indicators) and the eigenvector (used to calculate the
linear coefficient of water quality and socioeconomic indica-
tors) calculated in Step 2 are used to calculate the degree of
influence of each socioeconomic indicator related to water
quality; and (4) the comprehensive degree of influence of
socioeconomic development as it relates to water quality is
calculated by weighting and summing the corresponding de-
gree for each indicator in the third step. A detailed description
of this process was provided by Luo et al. ( 2019).
In this study, the degree of influence obtained from the
final calculation is divided into 10 levels, that is, no influence
(less than 0.1), level I (between 0.1 and 0.2), level II (between
0.2 and 0.3) and so on. The higher the level is, the greater the
degree of influence.

Relevance analysis of socioeconomic and river
systems
The typical correlation coefficients of YP1 –YP6 in the mon-
itoring sections of the Yangtze River Basin during the wet
season were 0.863, 0.973, 0.915, 0.885, 0.931 and 0.907, with
a mean value of 0.912. The typical correlation coefficients of
each section in the dry season were 0.773, 0.952, 0.886,
0.917, 0.789 and 0.918, with a mean value of 0.873. The
typical correlation coefficients of all sections in the Yangtze
River Basin in the above two periods were all greater than 0.6,
indicating a strong correlation between the socioeconomic
system and the water quality system in this area. The correla-
tion in the wet season was greater than that in the dry season in
the Yangtze River Basin. The typical correlation coefficients
of monitoring sections HP1–HP4 in the Yellow River Basin in
the wet season were 0.767, 0.882, 0.909 and 0.931, respec-
tively, with a mean value of 0.872. The typical correlation
coefficients of various sections in the dry season were 0.899,
0.992, 0.971 and 0.891, with a mean value of 0.938. There
was a strong correlation between the socioeconomic system
and water system in the Yellow River Basin. The correlation
in the dry season was generally greater than that in the wet
season in the Yellow River Basin.
The correlation range between socioeconomic indicators
and water quality indicators in all sections of the Yangtze
River Basin in the wet season was between –0.79 and 0.63,
and the correlation range in the dry season was between–0.84
and 0.7. The correlation ranges between systems in the same
period were 0.86–0.97 and 0.77–0.95, respectively. The cor-
relation range between socioeconomic indicators and water
quality indicators in various sections of the Yellow River
Basin in the wet season was between –0.86 and 0.74, and
the correlation range in the dry season was between –0.96
and 0.89 (Fig. 4). The correlation ranges between systems in
the same period were 0.77 –0.93 and 0.89–0.99, respectively.
The correlation between the systems in the two basins was
generally greater than those among the various indicators,
which indicates that the water quality in the two basins was
influenced by multiple factors. Luo et al. ( 2019) studied the

in Shaying River Basin. They found that the correlations be-

Conclusions
In view of the unbalanced economic development in the
Yangtze River Basin and the Yellow River Basin, we
analysed differences in the impacts of social and economic
development on water quality. From 2008 to 2017, the overall
degree of influence in the Yangtze River Basin exhibited
small fluctuations, whereas that in the Yellow River Basin
displayed a significant decline. The degree of influence also
exhibited seasonal variations. The water quality was generally
good. GDP per capita and urbanization rate are the main fac-
tors affecting the water quality of the Yangtze River Basin.
The main factor affecting water quality in the Yellow River
Basin is urbanization rate. The above results can help guide
the coordination of economic development and environmental
protection in the two basins in the future.
