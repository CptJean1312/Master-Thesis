# 2. Theoretical & Conceptual Background

## Overall job of Chapter 2
- Provide the conceptual backbone of the thesis before Chapter 3 turns to research design.
- Show that the thesis stands at the intersection of flood risk management, socio-spatial inequality, environmental justice, and socio-hydrology.
- Explain why the Elbe case is theoretically meaningful, not only empirically convenient.
- Build the conceptual bridge from theory to the empirical triad of `exposure - vulnerability - protection`.

## Red thread of the whole chapter
- Start with the institutional and governance context of flood risk management in Germany.
- Then narrow to the Elbe as a hydrological and socio-spatial case.
- Then introduce the normative lens: environmental and flood justice.
- Then explain why flood risk must be understood as a coupled social-hydrological process.
- End by bringing these strands together into the conceptual framing of the thesis.

## One-sentence chapter logic
- Because flood exposure and protection are shaped by governance, social structure, and coupled human-water dynamics, the distributive analysis of flood risk in the Elbe context requires both a justice framework and a socio-hydrological understanding of risk.

- Do **not** dump definitions without using them analytically.
- Do **not** turn the chapter into a generic flood textbook.
- Do **not** overstate causal claims; Chapter 2 should clarify concepts, not prove outcomes.
- Do **not** write a pure literature summary; always connect each subsection back to the thesis question.
